# Case Study Refactoring Protocol

Use this protocol to refactor, structure, and frame portfolio case study pages. The goal is to elevate standard portfolio copy into a world-class, high-impact narrative tailored for Design Executives (VP of Design, Head of Design), eliminating junior UX cliches and focusing on strategic leverage, system architecture, and business metrics.

## Execution Constraints
1. **Dynamic Headers Only:** Do NOT use the generic category names of the structural zones below as actual headers. Generate contextually distinct, unique, and professional section titles based entirely on the specific project's domain.
2. **Preserve Facts & Context:** Maintain the integrity of all core project details, user research insights, design evolution steps, usability testing scenarios, and business/operational metrics. **Do NOT omit key process steps or outcomes in the name of brevity; instead, weave them directly into the narrative structure with elevated, high-leverage phrasing.**

## The 4 Structural Zones

### ZONE 1: The Strategic Executive Summary [~60-second scan]
*Generate a sharp, domain-specific title focusing on the core mission.*
- **The Core Initiative:** A concise, powerful opening sentence defining your mandate, ownership, and project scale.
- **The Structural Paradox:** The friction point that made this highly complex (e.g., balancing non-deterministic AI outputs with reliable UX, or multi-tenant system constraints).
- **The Core Lever:** Your personal strategic contribution to unlocking the problem.
- **High-Impact Metrics & Project Outcomes:** A bolded summary line highlighting the organizational velocity, efficiency gains, financial transaction volume, and client/partner acquisition metrics.

### ZONE 2: Ecosystem Mapping & Systemic Strategy [The Macro View]
*Generate a title reflecting the high-altitude problem space (e.g., Tokenization, Frameworks, Architecture).*
- **Inherited Friction:** Define the exact systemic mess, legacy tech debt, or fragmented user experience you stepped into.
- **Systemic Research & Mental Models:** Detail the user research methodologies (expert interviews, card sorting, HMW sessions) and cognitive schemas used to categorize the chaos.
- **Cross-Functional Alignment:** Document how you negotiated trade-offs between Engineering constraints, Product goals, and Design quality to align the team around a unified taxonomy.

### ZONE 3: Technical Interaction Architecture [The Micro Deep-Dive]
*Generate a title focusing on the specific engineering or interaction challenge you solved.*
- **The Edge Case/Interaction Dilemma:** Zoom in on a highly technical, high-stakes micro-problem (e.g., token variable propagation, API latency handling, layout generator preview states).
- **The Iterative Evolution (Pivots & Testing):** Detail the design process iterations (e.g., transitioning from low-fidelity wireframes to high-fidelity dashboard variants), highlighting usability testing scenarios, user failures, and subsequent prototyping pivots.
- **The Definitive Pattern:** Showcase the final interaction pattern, describing exactly how it reduced user cognitive load or streamlined development handoff.

### ZONE 4: Force Multiplication & Governance [Leadership Impact]
*Generate a title centered on scale, mentorship, or organizational velocity.*
- **Operational Scalability:** Explain how the outputs of this project permanently upgraded the enterprise design system or introduced reusable code/design foundations.
- **Team Enablement & Launch Governance:** Detail how you successfully managed stakeholder buy-in, upskilled team members, and established strict quality gates (e.g., Design Gates) to scale execution across the department.
